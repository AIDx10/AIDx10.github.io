The documentary about Klyn workshop and Ukraine's grassroots drone revolution

About "Birds Flying East"

From artist to defender, teacher to humanitarian volunteer — this is Ukraine today. When Russia's invasion left Ukraine outgunned, citizens didn't wait for international support — they invented their own resistance. What began in kitchens and garages has exploded into a nationwide movement of collective action.

“Birds Flying East” is a documentary that follows the story of Kseniia Kalmus, a former florist from Kyiv who now leads Klyn, a civilian-run drone workshop where people from all walks of life come together to support Ukraine’s frontline defense. In Kseniia's workshop, engineers work alongside teachers, programmers with pensioners, students with artists — each bringing unique skills to a shared mission. This grassroots phenomenon represents Ukraine's most powerful weapon: the collective genius of ordinary people refusing to surrender their freedom.

Why This Movie Matters

"Birds Flying East" documents an unprecedented citizen response — a story of how Ukrainians united across all divides when faced with extinction, creating a new model of resistance that illustrates the importance of collective solidarity. This documentary is about the many invisible hands keeping Ukraine alive and we’d like to support their fundraising efforts by giving them platform and visibility.
