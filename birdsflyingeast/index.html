About "Birds Flying East"

From artist to defender, florist to innovator—this is Ukraine today. Our documentary captures how Kseniia Kalmus transformed from creating floral arrangements to leading the Klyn workshop, a powerful example of how Ukraine's grassroots resistance movement functions on the ground.

When russia's invasion left Ukraine outgunned, citizens didn't wait for international support—they invented their own resistance. What began in kitchens and garages has exploded into a nationwide movement that has revolutionized modern warfare. Today, Ukraine leads the world in drone innovation, with civilian workshops developing technologies that military powers now study and emulate.

In Kseniia's Kyiv-based Klyn workshop, engineers work alongside teachers, programmers with pensioners, students with artists—each bringing unique skills to a shared mission. This grassroots phenomenon represents Ukraine's most powerful weapon: the collective genius of ordinary people refusing to surrender their freedom.

"Birds Flying East" documents this unprecedented citizen response—a story of how Ukrainians united across all divides when faced with extinction, creating not just drones but a new model of resistance that may change defense strategies forever.
